\begin{abstract} 
To fully leverage mobile manipulation robots, agents must autonomously execute long-horizon tasks in large, unexplored environments \cite{ref1}. This paper addresses the challenge of interactive object search, where robots must navigate, explore, and manipulate objects (e.g., opening doors and drawers) to find targets in partially observable settings \cite{ref2}. We propose MoMa-LLM, a novel approach that grounds Large Language Models (LLMs) within structured representations derived from open-vocabulary scene graphs, which are dynamically updated during exploration \cite{ref3}. By tightly interleaving these representations with an object-centric action space, the system achieves zero-shot, open-vocabulary reasoning \cite{ref4}. To rigorously benchmark performance, we introduce a novel evaluation paradigm utilizing full efficiency curves and the Area Under the Curve for Efficiency (AUC-E) metric \cite{ref5}. Extensive experiments in simulation and the real world demonstrate that MoMa-LLM substantially improves search efficiency compared to state-of-the-art baselines like HIMOS and ESC-Interactive \cite{ref6}.
\end{abstract}

	\KwOutput{Placement pose \(\mathit{placePose}\).}

		}
	}
